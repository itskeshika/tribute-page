A Tribute to Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking was a world-renowned theoretical physicist and cosmologist.

Here is a short information about life of Hawking:

  • 8 January 1942: He was born in oxford into the family of physicians.
  • October 1959: At the age of 17, he began his university education at University College, Oxford, where he received a first-class BA degree in physics.
  • 1963: At age 21, Hawking was diagnosed with an early-onset slow-progressing form of motor neurone disease that gradually, over decades, paralysed him
  • 14 July 1965: Married to Jane Wilde whom he met at a party in their shared hometown st.Albanas.The couple had three children: Robert, born May 1967,Lucy, born November 1970,and Timothy, born April 1979
  • March 1966: He obtained his PhD degree in applied mathematics and theoretical physics, specialising in general relativity and cosmology.

Here are some achievements of Hawking:

  • 1970: Hawking postulated what became known as the second law of black hole dynamics, that the event horizon of a black hole can never get smaller.[90] With James M. Bardeen and Brandon Carter.
  • 1974: He found out that Black hole emit radiation known as Hawking's radiation,which may continue until they exhaust their energy and evaporate.
  • 1975: He was awarded both the Eddington Medal and the Pius XI Gold Medal
  • 1976: The Dannie Heineman Prize, the Maxwell Medal and Prize and the Hughes Medal.
  • 1979: Hawking was elected Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge
  • 1983: Published a model, known as the Hartleâ€“Hawking state. It proposed that prior to the Planck epoch, the universe had no boundary in space-time; before the Big Bang, time did not exist and the concept of the beginning of the universe is meaningless.
  • After 1985 to 1990: The Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society (1985),the Paul Dirac Medal and, jointly with Penrose, the prestigious Wolf Prize (1988). In the 1989 Birthday Honours, he was appointed a Companion of Honour.He reportedly declined a knighthood in the late 1990s in objection to the UK's science funding policy
  • 1993: He co-edited a book on Euclidean quantum gravity with Gary Gibbons and published a collected edition of his own articles on black holes and the Big Bang.
  • 2006: Hawking developed a theory of top-down cosmology, which says that the universe had not one unique initial state but many different ones, and therefore that it is inappropriate to formulate a theory that predicts the universe's current configuration from one particular initial state
  • 14 March 2018: He died in his home in Cambridge at the age of 76.

Why Hawking should be appreciated?( my personal view ) :

Hawking was a great physicist and cosmologist with a high intellect.Stephen Hawking made significant contributions to the field of theoretical physics and cosmology, and his work has had a lasting impact on our understanding of the universe. Some of the reasons why he is highly regarded and why people may be grateful for his contributions include:

  • He made groundbreaking discoveries in the field of general relativity, including the theoretical prediction of Hawking radiation, which demonstrated that black holes are not completely black.
  • He made significant contributions to the study of the Big Bang theory and the origins of the universe.
  • He helped to bring complex scientific ideas to a wider audience through his popular science books, including "A Brief History of Time," which became a bestseller and helped to increase public interest in science.
  • He was a passionate advocate for science education and the importance of scientific research.
  • Despite facing significant physical challenges due to his motor neuron disease, Hawking continued to work and make important contributions to his field, inspiring many people with his determination and spirit.
